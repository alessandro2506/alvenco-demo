# How Much Does a Website Cost in the UK? (2026 Complete Guide)

If you've ever Googled "website cost UK" and ended up more confused than before — you're not alone. Prices range from £99 on Fiverr to £50,000 from a London agency, and nobody seems to give a straight answer.

This article does.

---

## Why Website Prices Vary So Wildly

The short answer: **scope, complexity, and who's doing the work**.

A freelancer using a page builder quotes very differently from a specialist studio building custom, performance-optimised sites with proper SEO architecture. Both produce "a website" — but the outcomes are completely different.

Here's what actually drives cost:

- Number of pages and content complexity
- Bespoke design vs template
- Functionality — booking systems, payment gateways, AI integrations
- Integrations — CRM, ERP, email marketing, analytics
- Content production — copywriting, photography, video
- Ongoing maintenance — security updates, backups, hosting, support

---

## Website Cost Breakdown by Type (UK, 2026)

All figures are ex-VAT.

### 1. Landing Page — £700 to £1,800

A single-page site designed to convert visitors into leads or customers. Ideal for product launches, event registrations and lead generation.

### 2. Small Business Website — £1,400 to £3,500

3–6 pages covering Home, About, Services, Contact — sometimes with a blog.

> A well-built £2,000 website will outperform a £500 template site within 6 months on search rankings.

### 3. Corporate Website — £5,000 to £15,000

Multi-page sites with advanced functionality, custom UX, and content strategy.

### 4. E-Commerce — £3,200 to £14,000+

Budget £150–£300/month for hosting, security and platform updates on top of the build cost.

### 5. Web Application — £15,000 to £60,000+

Custom-built tools: booking platforms, SaaS dashboards, membership portals. This is software, not a website.

## Red Flags When Hiring a Web Agency

🚩 No written contract — walk away immediately

🚩 "Unlimited revisions" — exists to sound good, not to protect you

🚩 Full payment upfront — standard is 50% deposit, 50% on delivery

🚩 No fixed deadline in the proposal

🚩 Portfolio with no live URLs

🚩 They can't explain their tech stack
